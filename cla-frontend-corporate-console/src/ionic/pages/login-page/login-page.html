CLA Corporate Console

Authorize Your Corporate Developers

Ensure that your corporate developers are able to contribute code productively while streamlining how CLAs get signed by your company:

  • Sign Corporate CLAs using DocuSign® electronic signatures.
  • Authorize your developers under the project's Corporate CLA through one of the several ways: listing entire domains (@domain.com), GitHub handles, GitHub orgs or individual email addresses on a "Approved List".
  • Manage multiple projects

How to Start Using EasyCLA as a Corporate Contributor

Step 1

Set up your company on EasyCLA
  • The Project Manager, often the project maintainer, will have uploaded the appropriate CLA
  • When one of your developers attempts to contribute to the project, they will send a request for the CLA to be signed and that they ( and other developers ) be authorized to contribute.
  • Create your account to manage and sign the CLA.

Step 2

Sign the CLA
  • Either submit the address of your authorized signatory or sign the CLA directly yourself.
  • Signatures are captured electronically using DocuSign.

Step 3

Approve Your Developers

Once you have signed the CLA for the associate project, you can approve developers in several ways:

  • By Domain
  • By Github handle
  • By email address
